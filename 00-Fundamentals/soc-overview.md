\# SOC Overview



A Security Operations Center (SOC) is responsible for continuously monitoring, detecting, analyzing, and responding to cybersecurity threats within an organization.



\## Key Responsibilities

\- Monitor security alerts generated by SIEM tools

\- Analyze logs from endpoints, servers, firewalls, and network devices

\- Identify suspicious or malicious activity

\- Distinguish between false positives and real incidents



\## Role of a SOC Analyst

A SOC analyst investigates alerts, validates incidents, and follows incident response procedures. When a real threat is confirmed, the analyst escalates the incident according to severity and organizational policy.



\## Tools Used in a SOC

\- SIEM platforms (e.g., Splunk, Elastic, Sentinel)

\- Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)

\- Firewalls and IDS/IPS

\- Ticketing and case management systems



\## Skills Required

\- Networking fundamentals

\- Log analysis

\- Threat detection

\- Incident response

\- Documentation and reporting
